The Present

Who Is He Today & What Is He Doing?

Formerly A Redneck Texan Combat Soldier

To A Silicon Valley Sapper Coder

Retiring from the Army Feb 28th of 2018, I had a plan, my last few years in the service were rough, but I was set up to become a overseas contractor and then head to law school. Both of these prospects seemed respectable, but boring, and I was not looking forward to walking around in the desert getting shot at occassionally. I wanted adventure and action, but also friends, fun, brotherhood and women. The military had been my whole life starting military school at 13, then joining the army right after graduation, I knew nothing else.

The last possible thing I could ever think of doing was coming out to Silicon Valley to start a Software Company, then a Coding Boot Camp, and then transition into being a contractor for the military. So when my best friend John approached me with the idea, that is exactly what happened. We both dropped everything, I let law school and being a overseas contractor go to the wayside and said, "Lets do this".

I have always had a very strong belief that the path that is rarely traveled is the one that I must take. It is the hardest, most people do not know it many people think you are crazy for taking it, but it sure provides one hell of a adventure. What's more adventerous, difficult, stressful and fun then coming across the United States, to a place not meant for veterans in a entirely foreign environment and setting up a company? I sure can't think of anything but that's why I am here, and in time I know I will find more and more people like me around as well. The strivers, the risk-takers and ones who go all-in, those are my people, and if I can't find them here, I will bring them here myself, one veteran at a time.
